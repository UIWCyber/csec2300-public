What Is GRC?

  • Governance: who decides, and how decisions are directed and monitored (boards, committees, policies)
  • Risk management: the loop of identify, assess, treat, monitor (deep dive later in M10)
  • Compliance: proving you meet external obligations (laws, regulations, contracts)
DefinitionGRC = Governance, Risk, and Compliance. The "why" and "who says so" behind every technical control you will configure.
AnalogyGovernance is the captain setting the heading. Risk management is watching for icebergs. Compliance is the logbook that proves you followed maritime law.

The Policy Hierarchy

  • Policy: high-level intent, mandatory ("All code merged to main must be reviewed")
  • Standard: a specific mandatory requirement ("Reviews need 1 approver plus passing CI")
  • Procedure: the step-by-step how-to ("Open PR, request review, resolve comments, merge")
  • Guideline: recommended, not mandatory ("Prefer small PRs under 400 lines")
DefinitionThe difference is altitude. Policy says what and why, standard says how much, procedure says exactly how, guideline says what is wise.

Security Roles & Responsibilities

  • CISO / CSO: owns the program strategy and answers to the board
  • Security analyst / engineer: operates the controls day to day
  • Data owner: accountable for a data set's protection (sets the rules)
  • Data custodian / steward: implements that protection hands-on (follows the rules)
DefinitionAccountable vs. responsible: the owner is accountable (takes the blame), the custodian is responsible (does the work). Different people, different jobs.

Separation of Duties & Least Privilege

  • No single person should control an entire critical process: developer, approver, and deployer are different roles
  • GitHub makes this real: you cannot approve your own pull request, and CI deploys instead of humans
  • Collusion is the leftover risk. Job rotation and mandatory vacations act as detective controls
DefinitionLeast privilege = give each person the minimum access needed to do their job, nothing more.
AnalogyA bank does not let the same clerk both write the check and sign it. Two hands make fraud far harder.

Risk Vocabulary Starter Kit

  • Risk appetite: how much risk the organization is willing to accept overall
  • Risk tolerance: how much deviation is acceptable on a specific risk
  • Treatment options: mitigate (reduce it), transfer (insure it), avoid (stop the activity), accept (live with it)

Gap Analysis - From Framework to Roadmap

  • A framework tells you what "good" looks like. Gap analysis measures how far you are from it.
  • Step 1: choose a target profile (for example, CIS Controls IG1)
  • Step 2: assess your current state, control by control
  • Step 3: the delta between them is your prioritized roadmap
DefinitionGap analysis = compare where you are against where you want to be, and the difference becomes your to-do list.

Key Takeaways

  • GRC = direct, decide, prove. Governance directs, risk management decides, compliance proves
  • Policies cascade into standards, procedures, and guidelines. Altitude is the whole point
  • Roles matter: the owner is accountable, the custodian implements. Separation of duties stops single-actor failure
  • Gap analysis turns a framework into a prioritized plan
