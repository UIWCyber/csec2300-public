What Is Security? The CIA Triad

  • Security has three core goals. Almost every control you will ever meet protects one of them.
  • Confidentiality: only authorized people can read the data (encryption, access control)
  • Integrity: data and systems are not changed without authorization (hashing, signed commits)
  • Availability: the system works when you need it (redundancy, backups, defense against denial of service)

CIA in Action - Classify These

  • Ransomware encrypts a hospital's records and staff cannot open them. Which property failed?
  • An intern force-pushes over the main branch and the history is lost. Which property?
  • A misconfigured cloud bucket exposes 100 million records. Which property?
TipMany real incidents break more than one property at once. Always look for the second one.

Beyond CIA - AAA and Non-Repudiation

  • Authentication: prove who you are (password, key, MFA)
  • Authorization: what you are allowed to do once you are in
  • Accounting: a record of what you actually did
  • Non-repudiation: you cannot credibly deny an action you took
DefinitionNon-repudiation = proof of who did something, strong enough that they cannot deny it later. Git signed commits do exactly this.

In-Class Activity

CIA Triage (think, pair, share).

  1. 1Project six one-line incident headlines: breach, denial of service, website defacement, insider leak, ransomware, force-push over main.
  2. 2Solo, 2 minutes: tag each headline with C, I, and/or A.
  3. 3Pairs, 3 minutes: compare answers. Most incidents hit at least two properties. Find the second one.
  4. 4Debrief, 5 minutes: cold-call pairs. Emphasize the triad as an analysis lens, not trivia.
