Why Identity Matters Most Now

  • Cloud services and remote work dissolved the old network boundary; access now hinges on proven identity.
  • Most breaches start with credentials (phishing, password reuse, stuffing), not clever exploits.
DefinitionIAM = Identity and Access Management, the practice of governing who exists in a system, how they prove it, and what they may do.

The Three Factors

  • Something you know (password, PIN), something you have (phone, security key), something you are (fingerprint, face).
  • MFA means two or more DIFFERENT factors. A password plus a security question is still just one factor.
DefinitionMFA = Multi-Factor Authentication, requiring evidence from two or more different factor categories.
Watch outTwo things you know (password plus PIN) is not MFA. Both are the "know" factor and both fall to one phishing page.

Passwords and Passkeys Today

  • Length beats complexity; unique per site; a password manager is the practical control that makes both possible.
  • Passkeys are asymmetric key pairs, one per site: nothing shared to steal, nothing to phish.
AnalogyA passkey is your signed Git commit for logging in: you keep the private half, the site keeps the public half, and only you can produce the proof. This is the Lecture 12 payoff.

DAC, MAC, RBAC, ABAC

  • DAC: the resource owner decides (file permissions, GitHub repo collaborators).
  • MAC: the system enforces labels and clearances; owners cannot override (military systems, SELinux).
  • RBAC: permissions attach to roles, and users get roles (org teams: admin, maintain, write, read).
  • ABAC: policy evaluates attributes of user, resource, and context (time, device, location). Most flexible, most complex.

Least Privilege and Friends

  • Least privilege: the minimum access needed to do the job, reviewed and time-bound. JIT (just-in-time) access is the modern form.
  • Related principles: separation of duties, need-to-know, and deny-by-default.
  • You have already implemented all of these: branch protection, non-root containers, backend-only networks.
TipFor each control you built this semester, be able to name the principle behind it. That mapping is exam gold.

Worked Example - IAM on Your Capstone

  • Provision: a teammate is added as a repo collaborator with the write role, not admin.
  • Authenticate: MFA plus SSH keys. Authorize: branch protection limits what write can do to main.
  • Account: the audit log and signed commits. Deprovision: what happens when a teammate drops the class?

Key Takeaways

  • Authenticate the identity, authorize the action, account for everything, and deprovision on exit.
  • MFA quality varies: passkeys and FIDO2 beat TOTP, which beats SMS.
  • Know the four access models by their decider: owner (DAC), system (MAC), role (RBAC), policy and attributes (ABAC).
  • Sleep beats cramming. See you at the midterm.
