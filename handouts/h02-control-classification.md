# Handout H02: Control Sort Relay

**CSEC 2300 Foundations of Cyber Security | Dr. Gonzalo D Parra**
**Used with Lecture 2: Threat Actors & Frameworks | In-Class Activity: Control Sort Relay**

## Instructions

1. Form groups of four.
2. **Sort (6 minutes).** Your group gets the 12 controls below. For each control, write BOTH labels: its **category** (who or what enforces it) and its **function** (what it achieves). Place each control in the grid.
3. **Debrief (4 minutes).** The instructor reveals the intended answers. Argue the ambiguous ones. Ambiguity is normal; defend your reasoning.

**Reminders**

- **Categories:** Technical (systems enforce it), Managerial (decisions and policy), Operational (people and process), Physical (real-world barriers)
- **Functions:** Preventive (block it), Deterrent (discourage it), Detective (notice it), Corrective (fix it after), Compensating (substitute control), Directive (tell people what to do)
- One control can serve several functions. Classify by its *primary* purpose, then note the second one if it is strong.

## The 12 Controls

1. MFA on logins
2. Warning banner at login
3. CCTV cameras
4. Backup restore test
5. Security awareness training
6. Container image scanning in CI
7. Acceptable use policy (AUP)
8. Bollard in front of the entrance
9. IDS alert
10. Patching SLA
11. Branch protection on main
12. Security guard
