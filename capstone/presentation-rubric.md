# Capstone Presentation Rubric

**CSEC 2300 Foundations of Cyber Security** | Instructor: Dr. Gonzalo D Parra

The final presentation is worth 5% of your course grade (part of the 15% capstone). Teams present in posted time slots during the scheduled Capstone Presentations sessions (order posted on Canvas). Each team gets roughly ten to twelve minutes: about eight to present plus two or three for Q&A.

**Hard rules:**

- **Every member speaks.** Delivery credit requires a speaking part from each team member.
- **Missing your slot costs 15%.** Failure to present during the scheduled Capstone Presentations results in an automatic 15% deduction from the team's total capstone grade.
- **No late work.** The final repo, wiki, and board are due at the posted deadline on Canvas or they score zero.
- **Demo fallback required.** A recorded demo video must be in the repo. If the live demo fails, you switch to the recording and keep going; a working artifact is not penalized heavily for a failed cable.

## Required Structure (five beats, in order)

Every team walks the same five beats so scoring stays fair:

1. Architecture diagram plus a live demo of the running stack.
2. Controls mapped to course outcomes (CO) and SY0-701 domains.
3. One attack you defended (injection, an exposed service, and so on).
4. Risks accepted plus the incident-response note.
5. Lessons learned.

## Scoring Criteria and Weights

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Architecture and working demo | 25% |
| Security controls and mapping | 30% |
| Attack and defense narrative | 20% |
| Governance artifacts | 15% |
| Delivery and teamwork | 10% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

### Architecture and working demo (25%)

- **Excellent**: The stack runs live. Segmentation, TLS at the edge, and containerization are visibly demonstrated, not just claimed. The architecture diagram matches what is actually running.
- **Adequate**: The stack runs (live or via the required recording) and the diagram is accurate, but one or two hardening features are described rather than shown.
- **Missing or weak**: No working stack shown live or recorded, the diagram contradicts the deployment, or the stack is a stock install with no evident hardening.

### Security controls and mapping (30%)

- **Excellent**: Each implemented control is named, classified (preventive, detective, or corrective), and mapped to a specific CO and SY0-701 domain. The mapping is correct and the team can defend it under questioning.
- **Adequate**: Controls are listed and mostly mapped, but some classifications or domain mappings are vague, wrong, or unexplained.
- **Missing or weak**: Controls are named without mapping, the mapping is copied boilerplate the team cannot explain, or few real controls exist.

### Attack and defense narrative (20%)

- **Excellent**: A real attack is shown against the stack and its mitigation is demonstrated, with honest residual risk stated. "This injection still partially works; here is our compensating control" earns full credit. Honest security reasoning outranks a polished demo.
- **Adequate**: An attack and defense are described credibly but demonstrated only partially, or the residual risk discussion is thin.
- **Missing or weak**: No concrete attack, a purely hypothetical scenario with no evidence, or claims of perfect security with no residual risk acknowledged.

### Governance artifacts (15%)

- **Excellent**: The risk register, AI-use policy, and incident-response note are complete in the wiki, and the Git history and Project board show steady, distributed work all semester.
- **Adequate**: Artifacts exist but one is thin or stale, or the board and commit history show bursts rather than steady collaboration.
- **Missing or weak**: One or more artifacts absent, an empty or stale Project board, or commit history concentrated in a single member.

### Delivery and teamwork (10%)

- **Excellent**: Every member speaks with a substantive part. The talk is clear, hits the five beats in order, lands on time, and the team answers questions directly.
- **Adequate**: Every member speaks but parts are uneven, timing slips, or answers wander.
- **Missing or weak**: A member does not speak, the talk runs well over or under time, or questions go unanswered.
