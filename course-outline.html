1 Catalog Description

Introduction to the concepts of cyber security, including vulnerability assessment, virus attacks, hacking, spyware, network defense, passwords, firewalls, VPNs and intrusion detection. Crucial issues ranging from industrial espionage to cyber bullying are discussed.

2 Course Overview

The purpose of this course is to prepare students for roles as information security professionals. It provides the foundations of information assurance and security and introduces the breadth of domains needed for the CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) certification, which students continue toward in CIS 3353. Security concepts are taught through the modern tools of the trade: students practice version control and collaborative development with Git and GitHub, automate security checks with GitHub Workflows (CI/CD), isolate and harden services with Docker, study computer architecture with a focus on GPUs, and deploy and secure local AI systems (large language models, prompt/context engineering, and AI agents) using open-source software such as Ollama and Open WebUI.

Topics include: history of information systems security; security concepts and management; social engineering; compliance and regulations; policy management; risk assessment and ethical procedures; secure software development (Agile/DevSecOps); cryptography and identity & access management; trusted platforms and container isolation; and the security of AI systems.

5 Grading Activities, Criteria, and Guidelines

5.1 Grading Distribution

Your final grade is determined by autograded hands-on labs, quizzes, two exams, and a semester-long capstone project, weighted as follows:

  • Hands-on Labs (autograded via your assignment repository): 30%
  • Quizzes (Canvas): 15%
  • Midterm Exam: 15%
  • Final Exam: 20%
  • Capstone Project: 15%, broken down as follows:
    • Capstone Proposal: 3%
    • Final Presentation: 5%
    • GitHub Repository and Project Documentation: 4%
    • GitHub Wiki Page (Final Report): 3%
  • Attendance & Participation: 5%
  • Note on presentations: failure to present during the scheduled Capstone Presentations will result in an automatic 15% deduction from the team's total capstone grade.

5.2 Capstone Project Guidelines

The capstone is a semester-long team effort: build, harden, and document a secure local-AI stack (Dockerized Ollama + Open WebUI + an AI agent) delivered through a security-gated CI pipeline.

  • Team size: teams of 2 to 3 students. No solo capstones. Before committing to a team, compare schedules with your prospective teammates: you will collaborate and learn together all semester, so make sure you share workable meeting times.
  • Scope: Teams formally propose their configuration and target security controls; all proposals must be approved by the instructor.
  • Online Collaboration and Portfolio: Each team must create a GitHub Repository where all advancements and contributions are uploaded. The final report (as a GitHub Wiki page) and presentation materials must be housed in this repository. Add the following accounts as collaborators: gdparra-edu and cyberknowledge. Teams must use the integrated GitHub Project board to manage all project activities.
  • Submission: The links to your GitHub Repository and GitHub Project must be submitted through the corresponding Capstone Assignment on the Canvas portal.

5.3 Labs, Hints, and Automated Grading

  • Labs are distributed and pushed to your assignment repository; an automated grader runs on every push and reports your current score and per-criterion feedback, so you can revise before the deadline.
  • Every lab includes a three-tier hint ladder (nudge → guided → near-solution). Hints never change the grader: you earn what your submission proves.

5.4 Late Work and Make-up Policy

  • No late work is accepted in this course. All deliverables (labs, quizzes, papers, and every capstone component) must be submitted by the posted deadline; work not submitted on time receives a grade of zero for that item.
  • Extenuating circumstances: if a documented emergency prevents on-time submission, notify the instructor before the deadline whenever possible; accommodations are at the instructor's discretion and handled case by case.

5.5 Academic Integrity

All students are expected to adhere to the highest standards of academic honesty. Any act of academic dishonesty (e.g., cheating, plagiarism) will be handled in accordance with the procedures outlined in the UIW Student Handbook.

  • Students should refer to the Student Handbook (Section 5, Part D) for detailed definitions and procedures regarding academic integrity.

6.3 Attendance and Participation Policy

Attendance in this course is mandatory and essential for success. Active participation is a key component of the learning process and constitutes 5% of your final grade.

  • Absence Policy: You are permitted a maximum of three (3) absences during the semester without penalty, regardless of the reason (excused or unexcused). Upon your fourth absence, you will forfeit the entire attendance portion of your final grade.
  • Tardiness: Punctuality is expected. Arriving more than 10 minutes after class has started is considered tardy. Three instances of tardiness will be counted as one unexcused absence.
  • Official Excused Absences: For planned absences due to official university functions, religious holy days, or active military service, you must notify me in writing at least two weeks in advance, per university policy. For sudden illness or other extenuating circumstances, please notify me directly as soon as possible. While these absences are noted, they still count toward the three permitted absences for the semester.
  • Responsibility: Students who are not able to attend a course are responsible for dropping the course by the appropriate deadline. Per university policy, accumulating four or more absences without officially dropping the course will result in a failing grade.
